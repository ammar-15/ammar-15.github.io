## Inception

I basically had my **“screw this, I’m starting today”** moment in regard to starting my own content creation journey.

I’ve had it in the back of my mind for far too long to bury it and regret it later. One of my life’s goals is to **not have regrets when I die**, and missing out on content creation would have definitely made me have regrets.

After a year of delaying, **Ammar_tsx** was born on **[Instagram](instagram.com/ammar_tsx), [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@Ammar.tsx), and [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@Ammar_tsx)**.

## The Ancient YouTube Lore

I’ve always had a good interest for making skits or short plays, cinematography, editing videos, and photography.

In 2016, I started my own YouTube channel and gathered almost **40K views** in a span of 15 months, earning about **$60 in ad revenue**. It was during a time when YouTube was still in its golden age: non-mainstream, purely focused on YouTubers, and way before *“content creation”* became the label everyone used for it.

Although, the ad revenue never made it into me because YouTube’s AdSense payout threshold was **$100**. Later, YouTube revamped its monetization policy, and that $60 is now gone.

I don’t know where.

## Cringe, Peak, and the Pain of Not Continuing

Looking back, I do regret not making more content.

The content that looks cringe to me now obviously felt like **“Absolute Cinema,”** as people hype things these days. A 16-year-old boy getting **40K views** meant a lot to him in an era of YouTube where it was hard to get views in the same way short-form content allows today.

It’s the type of experience you gain only by **doing and evolving**. This can never be mastered by just *“learning”* from a book or a course.

## Content Creation: Born Again

Well, to stop having any more regrets, I decided to start again with full force.

I watched many videos and read a lot of blog posts to catch up on how platforms work these days. My outdated **2016 or 2020 info** wasn't going to cut it.

I read a book, suggested by my dear friend **[@lenxpedia](https://www.instagram.com/lenxpedia/)**, *Steal Like an Artist*. It’s definitely worth a read.

Hence, as the month of March dawned upon us, I had my [first reel](https://www.instagram.com/p/DVg93jTjy-6/) uploaded.

And it only fueled my fire to post more.

I am never disheartened by any reel that didn’t perform well. I just analyze it and learn from its mistakes.

## The Movie in My Head

My past self was happy that I finally had the ability to create content.

The content I really want to create is mainly **storytelling-style and cinematic**. It should feel like a movie, because I have always loved movies.

Its not close to what I want to achieve but thats the journey I will be getting on to reach my destination.

Also, a dilemma I had was this:

**Landscape content or vertical content?**

Now, I have posted some vertical Reels, but I really don’t like it. It’s fine for the Reels and topics I’ve posted. I’ve also always posted vertical content on **Paradigm’s Instagram** when I managed their social media, but that’s because it has its own brand identity compared to my own.

And I loved making [vertical content for Paradigm](https://www.instagram.com/reel/CzL_JbZuhxs/).

I just don’t like it for myself.

## The 4:3 Rebellion

I settled on **4:3**, and honestly, it’s slightly better than **16:9** for short-form content. I could even do **1:1 square content**, but 4:3 just hits better for me.

Yes, the big black bars exist.

But I don’t care.

This is going to be **my Instagram account**, and I can do whatever I like with it. If I decide to switch to vertical content later, that would be fine as well.

At the end of the day, if you aren’t satisfied with whatever work you’re putting in, what’s the point?

I love landscape videos and always have since I decided to post videos on YouTube back in 2016. So I’m going to continue it.

It is my niche.

It is my twist.

It is my way to spice things up.

As long as the content itself is good enough for the viewer to watch it completely, orientation doesn’t matter.
